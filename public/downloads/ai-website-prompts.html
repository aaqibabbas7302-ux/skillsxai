How to get consistent, publishable copy

LLMs follow structure. Give role, output shape, and constraints in one block. For regulated industries (health, finance, law), have a human review; these templates are starting points, not legal advice.

  • Fill every placeholder before generating; vague inputs yield generic text.
  • Chain prompts — outline first, then section-by-section, then line-edit pass with “improve for clarity, keep facts fixed.”
  • Ground with facts — paste bullet notes, not “make something up about our 10x ROI” unless you supply numbers.
  • Locale — add region, date format, currency, and English variant (e.g. en-IN vs en-US) for customer-facing text.
  • Style guide — add “avoid em dash; Oxford comma: yes; brand name casing: X” in a second system message or prefix.
  • Evaluation — keep 3 “golden” prompts; rerun after model or template changes to catch regressions.
  • Privacy — strip PII from prompts when using third-party APIs; use synthetic examples for public demos.
  • Accessibility — when generating for web, request heading hierarchy, link text that makes sense out of context, and alt text for meaning not decoration.

Common placeholder quick reference

Reuse your own short codes across templates (e.g. [ICP] = “solo founders running Shopify stores in India”) so iterations stay consistent.

TokenTypical meaning
[PRODUCT] / [PRODUCT_NAME]What you sell — app, SKU, or service name as customers see it.
[ICP]Ideal customer profile: role, industry, size, pain.
[CTA]Desired user action: verb-led (Start trial, Get sample).
[URL]Destination link; use UTM query params in your sheet, not in the prompt if you A/B the base URL.
[TONE] / [VOICE]adjective list + one competitor voice to match or avoid.
[KEYWORD]Primary SEO term; don’t stuff — one natural use per 100–150 words is a rule of thumb.
[PROOF]Named customer, NPS, star rating, or award — only if you can document it.
[BRAND]How the name is written; trademark symbol usage per your style guide.
[SEGMENT]CRM slice: new subscriber, high-LTV, trial ending, geography, plan tier.
[COMPLIANCE_NOTES]Claims you cannot make, disclaimers, age restrictions, or industry rules in plain text.

After the model responds — quality pass

  • Fact-check numbers, version names, and regulatory claims; replace hedging with verified sources or cut.
  • Read CTAs out loud: under 3 seconds for a mobile thumb zone; no duplicate “Learn more” without distinct destinations.
  • Scan for inclusive language; replace idiom that may not translate for your [LOCALE] audience.
  • SEO: one H1 per page, meta + visible copy aligned on intent, internal links to money pages and hub content.
  • Email/social: confirm [OFFER] and [DATE] match your CMS and store rules before scheduling.
  • Support macros: test links and placeholder substitution in Zendesk/Intercom before saving as a saved reply.
  • Brand: run a diff against your terminology sheet; ensure product names match your design system and package labels.

Re-run with “you are a skeptical editor; mark weak claims in [[brackets]]” for a second pass when stakes are high.
