Digital Marketing Essentials

Quick reference: strategy, channels, and measurement

1. SEO Fundamentals

SEO aligns content and site structure with how people search and how engines crawl. Think on-page (relevance), off-page (authority & trust), and technical (crawl, index, speed).

On-page

  • Match search intent (informational, navigational, transactional) before optimizing keywords.
  • One primary topic per URL; H1 = main topic; H2–H3 = supporting subtopics.
  • Title tag ~50–60 chars; meta description ~150–160 chars with CTA where natural.
  • Internal links with descriptive anchor text; fix orphan and thin pages.
  • Image alt text: concise, descriptive, not keyword-stuffed.

Off-page

  • Backlinks from relevant, reputable sites matter more than volume.
  • Brand mentions, reviews, and local citations support trust signals.
  • Digital PR, guest value (not guest spam), partnerships, and original research earn links.
  • Disavow only after genuine toxic link review—not as a default tactic.

Technical

  • Crawlable XML sitemap, clean robots.txt, canonical tags for duplicate URLs.
  • Core Web Vitals: LCP, INP, CLS—optimize images, JS, fonts, and layout shift.
  • HTTPS sitewide; fix 404s and redirect chains; structured data (JSON-LD) where it fits content.
  • Mobile-first: responsive layouts, tap targets, same primary content as desktop.

Keyword & topic research (practical flow)

  1. Start from customer questions and sales objections—not only volume tools.
  2. Map queries to intent and a target URL; avoid cannibalization (two pages for the same primary intent).
  3. Use Search Console, “People also ask,” forums, and support tickets for long-tail ideas.
  4. Score opportunities: business value × realistic ranking potential × CVR potential.
  5. Refresh high-traffic pages on a schedule (e.g. quarterly) with new stats, FAQs, and internal links.

E-E-A-T signals

  • Author bylines, bios, and credentials on YMYL topics.
  • Clear “about” and contact/address where appropriate.
  • Original data, first-party research, and cited sources.
  • Keep money pages updated; show last reviewed date for regulated topics.

Local SEO (if applicable)

  • GBP: categories, services, hours, photos, UTM-tagged website link.
  • NAP consistency; localized landing pages; review response cadence.
  • Local Business schema; location pages unique per branch.

2. Content Marketing Strategy

Map content to funnel stages, buyer jobs-to-be-done, and measurable outcomes—not just blog volume.

Type Best for Tip
How-to & guidesAwareness, SEOAnswer one query deeply; add FAQs from “People also ask.”
Case studiesConsideration, proofProblem → action → result with specific metrics.
Comparisons & toolsMid/bottom funnelBe honest; link to internal product where relevant.
Video & webinarsEducation, trustRepurpose into clips, quotes, and short posts.
  • Planning: Content pillars (3–5 themes) → topic clusters (pillar + supporting pages) → editorial calendar with owners and CTAs.
  • Distribution: Owned (site, email, app) first; then earned (PR, community); paid (boost best performers). Repurpose: one long piece → many formats.
  • Measure: Track assisted conversions, not just last-click; compare engagement by segment and by channel.

B2B vs B2C (mindset)

B2B B2C
Longer cycles; multiple stakeholders; prove ROI, risk, and implementation.Shorter path; emotion + offer clarity; social proof, urgency (used ethically).
Gated deep assets (e.g. RFPs, data sheets) + nurture.Lifestyle, UGC, reviews, seasonal hooks; D2C: retention programs.

Editorial quality bar (before publish)

  • One clear CTA; headline promises what the body delivers (no clickbait).
  • Scannable: subheads, bullets, bold key lines; add TL;DR on long B2B pieces.
  • Schema where relevant; internal links to product/money pages from educational posts.
  • Accessibility: alt text, heading order, color contrast, readable line length on owned pages.

4. PPC & Google Ads

Structure accounts for control and learning: clear campaigns → themed ad groups → tight keyword groups → tailored ads and landing pages.

Common campaign types

  • Search: High intent; use match types, negatives, and SKAGs/STRUC only if they simplify—often themed STAGs + smart bidding.
  • Performance Max: Cross-channel; needs conversion signals and feed/asset quality; monitor search terms and brand terms.
  • Display / Demand Gen: Awareness & remarketing; watch placements and frequency caps.
  • Video (YouTube): In-stream vs in-feed; use audience layering and creative variants.

Bidding (simplified)

  • Maximize conv. / tCPA: enough volume (30+ conv./mo) for learning.
  • tROAS: when revenue values are clean and consistent.
  • Start with reasonable targets; tighten after stable performance.

Optimization

  • RSAs: pin only when necessary; test 3–5 headlines, 2–4 descriptions.
  • Landing page = message match, fast load, one primary CTA.
  • Audit weekly: search terms, neg keywords, ad strength, quality components.

Match types & control (Search)

  • Exact and phrase give tighter control; broad match needs strong negative keyword hygiene + conversion data.
  • Mine search term reports: add negatives and new keyword themes weekly at first, then as volume dictates.
  • Separate brand from non-brand when reporting ROAS/CPA; avoid blending.

Google Ads Quality (practical levers)

Quality Score = proxy for ad relevance, landing page experience, and expected CTR. Improve by tightening keyword–ad–page alignment, not by chasing the number alone.

  • Split ad groups when intent or offer differs; write ads that mirror query language.
  • Mobile speed and CWV affect landing experience—fix before over-bidding.
  • Extensions: sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets—improve real estate and CTR when relevant.

Conversion tracking checklist

  1. Primary vs secondary goals defined; values for revenue where possible (even estimates beat empty).
  2. Tag Manager: test in GTM preview; use consent mode if EU traffic.
  3. Offline conversions: CRM sync or manual import for B2B lead quality.
  4. Remarketing: audience lists (GA4, customer match where eligible), list sizes before activation.
  5. Attribution: document whether reports use data-driven, last click, or modeled views.

5. Email Marketing

Email still delivers strong ROI when permission-based, relevant, and automated where it reduces friction.

  • Subject lines: Clarity > hype. Front-load the benefit. 40–50 chars for mobile. A/B test: tone, length, numbers, personal vs generic.
  • Preheader: Complements the subject—don’t repeat it. Use to add context or a secondary benefit.
  • Body: One goal per email; scannable; single primary CTA above the fold; plain-text alternative for important comms.
Segmentation Examples
BehaviorBrowse/cart, content consumed, app activity, last purchase.
Demographic / firmographicLocation, plan tier, company size, role (B2B).
EngagementOpens/clicks, VIP vs lapsing, sunset rules for inactives (re-engagement, then stop).

Automation ideas: welcome series, post-purchase onboarding, browse/cart recovery, win-back, milestone triggers. Always monitor deliverability: SPF, DKIM, DMARC, list hygiene, and complaint rate.

High-performing flow patterns

Flow Moves the needle when…
Welcome (3–5 emails)You set expectations, deliver value fast, and segment follow-up by interest.
Browse / cartCreative shows exact products viewed; inventory-aware; 2–3 touches max to avoid annoyance.
Post-purchase / onboardingReduces refunds/churn, drives reviews, and upsell only after success moment.

Compliance (don’t ship without)

  • CAN-SPAM (US): honest From/Subject, physical address, visible unsubscribe, process opt-outs within 10 business days (verify current law).
  • GDPR / ePrivacy (EU/UK): consent where required, clear purposes, easy withdrawal; data processing agreements with ESP.
  • Apple MPP & privacy: open rates skew high—rely on clicks, on-site events, and revenue for performance.

6. Analytics & KPIs

Define a North Star or funnel KPIs per channel. Compare cohorts, don’t only report totals. Document UTMs and conversion definitions so everyone agrees what “conversion” means.

Key metrics to track (examples)

  • Traffic: sessions, users, new vs returning, channel mix (GA4), landing page performance.
  • Engagement: engagement rate, average engagement time, scroll depth (events), video completion.
  • Conversion: goal completion rate, form abandonment, assisted conversions, path exploration.
  • Commerce: AOV, cart conversion, repeat purchase rate, CAC, LTV (or proxy).
  • Paid: CPC, CPM, CPA, ROAS, MER (total ad spend / total revenue) for holistic view.
  • Email: open rate (device-dependent), click rate, conversion rate, unsubscribe, spam complaints, deliverability (bounce types).

Tools (typical stack): GA4 + Tag Manager, Search Console, ad platforms, CRM/email (HubSpot, Klaviyo, etc.), hotmaps/session replay for UX, and a spreadsheet/BI tool for MER and blended reporting. Align on one source of truth per metric in leadership reviews.

GA4 event ideas (examples)

  • generate_lead, purchase with value and currency.
  • Engagement: scroll 75%+, video milestones, file download, click_outbound, phone_click.
  • UTM discipline: lowercase, consistent naming; no spaces; document in a shared sheet.

Conversion & growth (mini)

CRO habits

  • Hypothesis → one change → measure; avoid multivariate chaos on low traffic.
  • Above-the-fold clarity: offer, proof, CTA; remove navigation noise on paid landers when safe.
  • Form fields: ask only what you need; inline validation; trust badges below CTA.
  • Speed: defer non-critical JS; compress hero media; preconnect to font CDNs if used.

Formulas to know

  • CTR = clicks ÷ impressions
  • CPC = ad spend ÷ clicks
  • CPA = ad spend ÷ conversions
  • ROAS = revenue ÷ ad spend
  • MER = total marketing spend ÷ total attributed revenue (channel-agnostic health)
  • List growth = (new subs − (unsub + bounces + complaints)) ÷ list size (period)

Benchmarks vary wildly by industry—use cohorts and YoY before comparing to generic blog stats.

Testing, ops & brand safety

Ship small experiments; document learning even when the variant loses—knowledge compounds.

Test type What to change Read results
A/B (site)Headline, hero CTA, form length, social proof orderPrimary conversion + guardrail (bounce, RPV)
A/B (email)Subject, preheader, send time (segmented), layoutClick + revenue per send (not open alone)
Creative (ads)Hook, offer framing, UGC vs polished, 6s vs 15sCPA/ROAS with min. conv. for stability
  • Sample size: Use a calculator (baseline rate, MDE, power); don’t call tests early on thin traffic.
  • Holdouts: For heavy discounts, run small holdout groups to see true incrementality when feasible.
  • Brand & PR: Pre-approved response matrix for product issues; monitor @mentions, reviews, and Google autocomplete risk.

Funnel, retention & LTV (aligned metrics)

Acquiring users is only half the system—activation, retention, and expansion often beat another point of ROAS in mature markets.

Common funnel stages (adapt labels to your org)

  1. Awareness — Impressions, branded search lift, new visitors by channel.
  2. Consideration — Engaged sessions, return visits, lead magnet or demo requests.
  3. Conversion — SQLs, signups, first purchase, trial start.
  4. Retention & expansion — DAU/WAU, churn, NRR, cross-sell, repeat order interval.

For SaaS, pair marketing metrics with product activation (e.g. time-to-value). For e-commerce, watch margin after discount and return rate, not just MER.

Glossary (quick)

AOV — average order value; revenue ÷ orders.

CAC — customer acquisition cost; sales+marketing ÷ new customers in period (align to payback model).

CLA / CLS — cumulative layout shift; CWV component for visual stability.

CTA — call to action; one primary per unit of attention.

CWV — Core Web Vitals (LCP, INP, CLS).

E-E-A-T — experience, expertise, authoritativeness, trustworthiness.

GBP — Google Business Profile (local listings).

INP — interaction to next paint; responsiveness CWV.

KPI — key performance indicator; tie to decision & owner.

LCP — largest contentful paint; loading CWV.

LTV — lifetime value; profit-based preferred when available.

MER / ER — marketing efficiency; total spend over revenue (names vary by team).

NRR — net revenue retention; expansion vs churn in cohort.

PPC — pay per click; paid search/social when billed on clicks (broader: paid media).

PMAX — Performance Max (Google) cross-channel with asset groups.

RSA — responsive search ad; multiple assets combined by the engine.

SEO / SEM — organic vs paid search (SEM often = paid, verify team dialect).

SQL / MQL — sales- vs marketing-qualified lead; definitions must be written down.

UTM — campaign URL parameters for source, medium, campaign, content, term.

YMYL — your money your life; stricter E-E-A-T expectations.

Weekly marketing health check (quick)

  1. Top traffic & conversion changes vs last week (and same week prior year if seasonal).
  2. Search Console: impressions/clicks, critical errors, new queries worth content.
  3. Paid: wasted spend, new negatives, creative fatigue, budget pacing.
  4. Email: list growth vs churn, deliverability, top flows by revenue.
  5. Social: what creative hit; comments/DMs needing response; UGC to reshare.
